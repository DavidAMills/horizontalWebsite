Step 1: Collect Data and Engage the Public

This section describes methods for capturing and understanding existing pedestrian safety trends and engaging stakeholders.

Pedestrian Crash Data Collection

Collect and analyze data on pedestrian crashes to identify problem areas and improve safety measures.

Review Existing Highway Safety Plans

Analyze current highway safety plans to understand existing policies and their impact on pedestrian safety.

Initiate a Pedestrian Safety Action Plan

Develop and implement an action plan specifically focused on improving pedestrian safety.

Pedestrian Safety Policy Analysis

Assess current policies that affect pedestrian safety and identify areas for improvement.

Request and Receive Public Input

Engage with the public to gather insights, feedback, and suggestions on pedestrian safety initiatives.

Conduct a Walkability Audit

Evaluate pedestrian infrastructure to identify barriers and opportunities for enhancing walkability.

Step 2: Inventory Conditions and Prioritize Locations

This Studio section describes the options for how an agency can document field conditions (such as roadway characteristics) and screen the transportation network for high-crash and high-risk locations. An agency can perform any or all of these analyses.

Inventory Roadway Characteristics and Pedestrian Crossings

Document field conditions, such as roadway characteristics and pedestrian crossings, to identify high-risk areas.

Conduct a Crash Cluster Analysis

Analyze crash data to identify clusters of high-crash locations, helping to prioritize areas for safety improvements.

Conduct a Systemic Analysis

Screen the transportation network for high-risk locations by identifying common risk factors associated with crashes.

Step 3: Analyze Crash Types and Safety Issues

This Studio section describes methods for summarizing pedestrian crash types and observed traffic safety issues. This information is important for selecting countermeasures.

Create Crash Diagrams

Visualize crash data through diagrams to better understand the types and patterns of pedestrian crashes.

Identify Crash Factors

Analyze contributing factors to pedestrian crashes, such as vehicle speed, road conditions, and pedestrian behavior.

Conduct a Pedestrian Road Safety Assessment (RSA)

Perform a Road Safety Assessment to systematically evaluate pedestrian safety issues and recommend countermeasures.
